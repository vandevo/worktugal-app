What is happening
Mistral AI, the Paris-based competitor to OpenAI and Anthropic, has initiated a significant hiring push after reaching a $14 billion valuation in September 2025. Backed by a $3 billion war chest from investors like Nvidia and Microsoft, the company is recruiting for over 125 positions to build sovereign AI infrastructure and enterprise tools.

The hiring surge
Mistral AI currently lists over 125 open roles. The majority are based at the headquarters in Paris, with additional hubs in London and Amsterdam. The company is scaling rapidly to convert its $3 billion cash reserves into technical dominance, specifically targeting infrastructure, model safety, and enterprise product development.

Why it matters
This recruitment drive represents one of the few “rocket ship” opportunities in the European tech sector right now. While US giants dominate the market, Mistral is positioning itself as the sovereign EU alternative.

For candidates, this offers a rare chance to work on frontier models (like Mistral Large 2) without moving to San Francisco. The company’s stability is bolstered by its recent $2 billion Series C raise, providing a long runway despite the capital-intensive nature of training large language models.

Roles and compensation
The open positions are heavily skewed toward technical specialization. Key areas include:

High-Performance Computing (HPC): Engineers to build the planned 1.4 GW AI campus.
AI Safety & Alignment: Researchers to mitigate reported vulnerabilities in open-weight models.
Product & Deployment: Managers to integrate Mistral’s API with enterprise tools like Snowflake and Asana.
Pay transparency warning:
Mistral AI does not publish salary ranges on its job listings. This is a significant blind spot for applicants. Based on market data for similar Series C deep-tech firms in Paris and London, senior engineering roles typically command base salaries between €100,000 and €180,000.

Because the company is valued at $14 billion, equity packages will be a critical part of the offer. Candidates must negotiate based on total compensation, not just base salary.

Analysis: The risk and reward
The upside at Mistral is clear: high-growth equity and the chance to build Europe’s primary AI infrastructure. The company has reported zero layoffs since its 2023 founding, signaling organizational stability.

However, the HR infrastructure appears to lag behind the technical ambition. There is no public information regarding visa sponsorship or relocation packages, despite the company’s stated desire for top global talent. Furthermore, the remote work policy is inconsistent—some roles are “fully remote” while others require strict on-site presence, with no centralized documentation to clarify the rules.

How to apply
Applications are processed through the company’s Lever portal.

Review the stack: Ensure your CV highlights experience with LLM infrastructure or open-source model deployment.
Prepare for opacity: Go into the interview ready to ask specific questions about visa support and remote flexibility, as these are not detailed online.
Anchor your rate: Since ranges are hidden, research local market rates for “Staff Engineer” or “Lead Product Manager” in Paris to avoid lowball offers.
Local terms to know
CDI (Contrat à Durée Indéterminée): The standard permanent employment contract in France, offering strong worker protections.
BSPCE: A specific type of stock option for French startups, often with favorable tax treatment. Ask if your equity grant falls under this category.
Common traps
Assuming remote means global: “Fully remote” often implies “remote within the country of employment” for tax reasons. Do not assume you can work from Portugal for a Paris role without written confirmation.
Ignoring the safety reports: Recent audits showed Mistral models had higher rates of harmful content generation. Safety engineers should view this as a core job responsibility, not just a PR issue.
Overvaluing paper money: A $14 billion valuation is high. Ensure you understand the strike price of your options and the conditions for liquidity.
Bottom line
Mistral AI offers the highest-profile tech jobs in Europe right now, backed by massive capital. The trade-off is a lack of HR transparency regarding pay and relocation. Apply if you want high-stakes technical work, but negotiate your terms aggressively.
