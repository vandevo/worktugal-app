IKEA Boosts Pay and Benefits for Portugal Staff: Minimum Wage Jumps to €1,025 in 2025 💰
Swedish retailer IKEA increases minimum wage by 2.5%, adds to benefits like 26 vacation days and a meal allowance, investing €5M.
Van Vo
Dec 18, 2024

IKEA Portugal is set to increase its minimum entry-level salary by 2.5% to €1,025 gross per month and will provide a meal allowance of €8 per day for full-time staff, effective in 2025. The retailer is investing approximately €5 million into salary increases and benefits, which also include 26 vacation days for employees. This move follows a prior investment of €8.5 million in 2024.

This significant financial commitment reflects the company's effort to attract and retain talent, providing a competitive edge for workers.




The investment demonstrates IKEA's commitment to its employees' well-being and financial security.
IKEA's Commitment to Employee Welfare
The Swedish retailer has announced that they will be increasing the minimum wage for entry-level positions to €1,025 per month, a 2.5% increase from 2024. This rise is paired with a meal allowance increase to €8 per day for full-time workers. In addition to these financial benefits, employees will now receive a total of 26 vacation days annually. This move is part of a broader investment plan of €5 million for 2025, focusing on enhancing both salaries and extra-salary benefits. This is not the first time IKEA has focused on its staff. In 2024 the retailer invested €8.5 million into salary increases and performance bonuses, signaling a long-term commitment to staff.

Eliska Novotná, the country people & culture manager at IKEA Portugal, stated, “At IKEA, the constant improvement of our employees’ conditions is a top priority, and it has been happening consistently year after year. After two years of continuous investment in our people, we will again increase the entry-level salary without compromising our capacity to reward performance and competence of employees.” This statement highlights the company’s dedication to its workforce.

Comprehensive Benefits Package
IKEA provides more than just competitive wages. The company offers a variety of additional benefits, including:

Parental Support: A "Birth Aid" of €870 gross per child for biological and adoptive parents.

Extended Parental Leave: A program to prolong parental leave by two months, beyond the standard social security period, with a monthly payment of €870 gross, up from €820 last year.

Health and Life Insurance: Comprehensive health and life insurance for all permanent employees.

On-Site Medical Support: Internal medical support for the staff.

Emergency Social Support: A program to support employees during emergencies.

Affordable Meals: An exclusive staff restaurant offering low-priced meals.

IKEA Portugal also reports that over 90% of its 3,000 employees are on permanent contracts. Furthermore, the company emphasizes that there is no salary disparity between men and women in equivalent roles. Women occupy 50% of the leadership positions.

Impact and Future
These improvements are designed to boost employee satisfaction and ensure that IKEA remains an attractive employer in Portugal. The company has consistently raised its minimum salary, moving from €750 in 2022 to €1,000 in 2023, and now to €1,025 in 2025. This puts their minimum wage well above the national minimum wage, set to be €870 in 2025. These adjustments, along with the increased meal allowance and extra benefits, are intended to reward employees' daily dedication over the past 20 years.

The investment demonstrates IKEA's commitment to its employees' well-being and financial security. The increase in vacation days and meal allowances, combined with other benefits like parental support, underscores the company's aim to provide a supportive work environment. These changes will affect the 3,000 IKEA employees, with the majority on permanent contracts, signaling the company’s stability.

Next Steps
For job seekers, IKEA's increased minimum wage and expanded benefits make it an attractive employer. For current staff, these changes mean greater financial security and support. Companies can learn from IKEA's employee-centric approach, which could result in increased loyalty and productivity. This is a clear example of how investing in employees can strengthen a business.
