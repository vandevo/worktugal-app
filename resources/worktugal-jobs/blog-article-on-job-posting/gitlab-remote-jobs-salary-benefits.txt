Benefits and Perks at GitLab
GitLab offers a comprehensive benefits package that caters to its employees’ well-being and professional growth. Here are some of the key benefits:

Health, Vision, and Dental Insurance: 100% coverage for employees and 66% coverage for family members.
Employee Assistance Program (EAP): GitLab provides access to an EAP through Modern Health, offering support to all team members.
Unlimited Time Off: GitLab operates on a “no ask, must tell” policy, allowing employees to take unlimited time off, with a minimum of 25 days per year.
401(k) and Flexible Spending Account (FSA): GitLab offers a 50% match on the first 6% of base salary, with a yearly cap of $1,500. Company contributions vest evenly over four years.
Life Insurance: GitLab provides a $20,000 lump sum to the designated beneficiary of any team member who passes away.
Parental Leave: GitLab offers up to 16 weeks of paid parental leave for employees who have been with the company for at least six months and completed their probationary period. This applies to childbirth or adoption events.
GitLab Contribute: GitLab hosts an annual team event, GitLab Contribute, where employees from across the globe gather to interact, network, and strengthen the community. Attendance is optional.
Growth and Development Benefit: GitLab supports employee professional development by reimbursing up to $10,000 USD per calendar year for educational expenses, subject to tenure, performance, company need, and budget availability.
