Welocalize, a major player in translation and AI data services, is actively recruiting for hundreds of remote roles across the globe. The company is seeking linguists, content reviewers, AI data raters, and quality testers to support its contracts with large technology companies. The positions are available in numerous countries, including the United States, United Kingdom, Spain, Portugal, Germany, Romania, India, Japan, and across Latin America.

This large-scale hiring effort is driven by the booming demand for human-powered data to train and refine artificial intelligence models. Welocalize acts as a key supplier of this labor, providing the workforce needed for tasks like search quality rating, ad evaluation, and content localization. For job seekers, this offers a direct entry point into the remote gig economy supporting the AI industry.

What the roles involve
The available positions are a mix of freelance, part-time, and full-time contracts, with many roles centered on project-based tasks.

Key remote job categories include:

AI Data Rater / Search Quality Rater: Evaluating the quality and relevance of search engine results, ads, and other AI-generated content.
Linguist / Translator: Translating, reviewing, and localizing content for specific regions, including technical patents, marketing copy, and software.
Content Reviewer / Metadata Specialist: Checking video, music, or app content for quality, accuracy, and cultural appropriateness.
AI/Machine Learning Engineer: Technical roles focused on developing and managing AI platforms and data pipelines.
Pay structures vary widely. Employee reviews and job postings indicate that US-based roles like Search Quality Rater often pay around $15 per hour. However, compensation for roles outside the US is frequently lower and may be calculated per task rather than per hour. The work is known for its flexibility, allowing contributors to set their own schedules, but the availability of tasks can be inconsistent.

All applications are managed through the official Welocalize careers portal.

Common traps (and how to avoid them)
Based on extensive applicant and employee feedback, candidates should be aware of several common issues:

Inconsistent work. Many roles, especially for raters, experience periods of “No Tasks Available” (NTA). This makes it an unreliable primary source of income for many.
Unpaid training and assessments. The onboarding process can be lengthy, often requiring applicants to study dense guideline documents and pass multiple exams on their own time.
Pay disparity. Compensation differs significantly between the US and other global markets. Do not assume a US-based hourly rate applies to a role in another country.
Subjective quality reviews. Performance is measured against strict and sometimes subjective quality standards. Failing to meet these standards can lead to account suspension or termination with little warning.
Slow communication. As a large, distributed organization, getting support from HR or project managers can be slow, with some describing the experience as “your boss is a PDF.”
Bottom Line
Welocalize offers a legitimate and highly flexible opportunity to earn money remotely by contributing to the AI industry. It is best suited for individuals seeking a supplemental income or a side gig rather than a stable, full-time career with predictable pay.

FAQ
Are these full-time jobs?
The openings are a mix of full-time, part-time, and freelance contract roles. Many are project-based.

Is the work consistent?
No. Many contributors report periods with no available tasks, which can affect weekly and monthly income.

Is the pay competitive?
Pay varies greatly by location. While flexible, many reviews state the compensation is lower than industry averages for similar work, especially for non-US roles.

Is training paid?
The initial study and assessment period required to qualify for a role is typically unpaid.
