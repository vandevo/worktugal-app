About Cloudflare
Cloudflare is a global technology company that operates one of the world’s largest networks, powering over 25 million internet properties. They provide a suite of services that protect and accelerate websites and applications, including security, performance, reliability, and insights. Cloudflare is known for its commitment to building a better internet and fostering a diverse and inclusive workplace.

Cloudflare’s Mission
Cloudflare’s mission is to help build a better internet. They believe in protecting the free and open internet, empowering organizations and journalists with powerful tools to defend themselves against attacks. Cloudflare also actively contributes to projects like the Athenian Project, ensuring secure access to election information for citizens, and the Path Forward Partnership, providing mid-career professionals with opportunities to re-enter the workforce.

Cloudflare’s History
Cloudflare’s story began with Project Honey Pot, a system created in 2004 to track and combat email spam. In 2009, Matthew Prince, Michelle Zatlyn, and Lee Holloway came together to develop a service that would not only track but also stop internet threats. Cloudflare was born. The company quickly gained traction and secured funding from top investors, attracting talented engineers from leading tech companies like Google, Yahoo, and PayPal.

Cloudflare’s Growth
Cloudflare has grown rapidly since its launch in 2010. They have opened six offices across three countries, brought 300 data centers online, and launched dozens of products and hundreds of features. Their focus on performance, security, reliability, and insights has earned them a loyal customer base, ranging from individual bloggers to Fortune 500 companies.
