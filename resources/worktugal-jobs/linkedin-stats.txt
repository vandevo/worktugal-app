Most EU citizens moving to Portugal think the passport handles everything.

It handles the right to be there. That's it.

CRUE registration, NISS, SNS access, IRS filing, IFICI. Those are four separate registrations with different offices, different deadlines, and real penalties if you miss them.

The one that costs people the most: CRUE. EU citizens must register at their Câmara Municipal within 30 days of hitting the 90-day mark. Most either don't know it exists or walk into AIMA, which handles non-EU nationals and will redirect you. Fine for missing the deadline: €400 to €1,500.

The one with the permanent consequence: IFICI. NHR ended January 1, 2024. IFICI (NHR 2.0) replaced it with narrower eligibility and a deadline of January 15 of the year after you become tax resident. Miss it once and it's gone for good.

And pensions are no longer protected. Under old NHR, 10% flat. Under IFICI, standard IRS rates up to 48%+. If you moved to Portugal specifically to retire under NHR, this changes the math completely.
