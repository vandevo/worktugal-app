Van Vo, I think you’re right to call out the problem, but here’s the flaw in the way this policy is being sold.

If 75% of rents are already under €2,300, then giving landlords a tax cut at that level isn’t “moderation.” It’s just handing them money for what they were already charging. Tenants see no relief, because nothing forces prices down. It’s essentially a subsidy disguised as rent policy.

And let’s be real: the median salary in Portugal is nowhere near enough to make €2,300 a “moderate” rent. Most people earn around €1,000–€1,200 a month, and they’re already paying heavy taxes on top of that. For a tenant, rent can swallow an entire income before utilities or groceries even enter the picture.

So the real danger isn’t just landlords aiming for €2,300. It’s that the state itself has rebranded that number as “moderate”. Once you normalize that figure, you move the goalposts far away from what ordinary households can ever afford.
