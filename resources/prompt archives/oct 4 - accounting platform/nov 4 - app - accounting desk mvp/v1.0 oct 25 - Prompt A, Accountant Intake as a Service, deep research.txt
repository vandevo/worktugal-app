Prompt A, Accountant Intake as a Service, deep research

You are my senior market analyst. Objective, validate and spec a B2B product called Accountant Intake as a Service for Portugal focused on freelancers and remote workers. Deliver a concise, source-linked brief with tables.

Scope and constraints

Timeline window, 2024 to today. Prioritize Portugal content in Portuguese and English.

Trust sources, OCC and TOConline docs, Contabilista.pt directory, Finanças portal, AIMA updates, reputable firms with price pages, Reddit PortugalFinance, r/expats, Google reviews with dates.

Exclude vague blogs that do not cite law or show prices. Flag any claim that lacks a source.

Tasks
A. Market map
• List 12 small to mid Portuguese accounting firms and 3 immigration boutiques that serve expats or freelancers. Provide website, price ranges for consults or onboarding, time to first consult, languages, and whether they show an intake form.
• Note their bottlenecks that intake could fix, missing docs, language barrier, response time, invoice errors, Finanças portal access.

B. Demand signals
• Extract 20 pains from real user posts and dated reviews that mention accountant ghosting, missing invoices, e-fatura confusion, NIF or fiscal representative timing, NISS, AIMA requirements, VAT number setup, IRS Category B starts. Quote short excerpts with dates and links.
• Identify any willingness to pay for short paid consults or intake services. Capture price points.

C. Compliance backbone
• Summarize the minimum facts an intake must capture to hand off a clean file. Fields for identity, residence, tax status, NIF, NISS, IBAN, activity code, invoicing platform, e-fatura usage, prior IRS year, VAT regime, fiscal representative status, electronic notifications. Cite legal or institutional references where available.

D. Existing substitutes
• Compare 10 services that overlap intake, examples, Fresh Portugal consults, GoalSeek, Migrun, E-Residence, Novomove, Rauva, GetNIF, boutique accountants. Table fields, product focus, price, delivery time, refund, languages, what intake they already do, and gaps.

E. Pricing and unit economics
• Find concrete prices for consults, onboarding, or document preparation. Propose two B2B pricing models for intake only.
Model 1 subscription, 149 per month for up to 10 intakes then 15 per extra.
Model 2 pay per qualified intake, 39 per completed handoff package.
• Estimate hours saved for a firm per client if intake removes two back-and-forth cycles. Support with any source or a conservative assumption clearly labeled.

F. Delivery design and risks
• Define the minimum credible backbone, intake form fields, file checklist, 48-hour SLA, weekly handoff report schema.
• List five failure modes and preventions, identity mismatch, incomplete tax history, IBAN verification, language misread, VAT regime errors.

G. Pilot and outreach plan
• Draft a 14-day pilot with pass or fail gates. Success metric, at least 6 of 10 intakes delivered within SLA and one renewal signal.
• Provide a 7-day outreach plan, where to find targets on Contabilista.pt, what to say, and two email scripts of three lines each with a prepaid Stripe link and a 15 minute kickoff booking.

Output format

Executive summary, 5 lines.

Tables, competitors and prices, accountant targets, substitute services.

Compliance checklist, bullet list of fields and links.

Pilot design and outreach scripts.

Sources, numbered with dates.
