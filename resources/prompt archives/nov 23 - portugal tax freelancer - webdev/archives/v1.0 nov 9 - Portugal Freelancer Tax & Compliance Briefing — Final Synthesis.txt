KEY FINDINGS & CLARITY DELIVERED
The System in 30 Seconds:
Portugal's freelancer tax system (Simplified Regime under €200k) is simple if you follow the rules on time; expensive if you don't.

Registration: NIF → Bank Account → Activity Registration (abrir atividade) → NISS (4–10 weeks)

Ongoing: Issue recibos verdes per payment, file quarterly social security reports (even Year 1), monitor VAT threshold €15,000

Filing: Annual IRS between April 1–June 30 (or €200–€2,500 fine + interest)

Tax Burden: Year 1 = ~14% (IRS only, SS exempt); Year 2+ = ~29% (IRS + Social Security combined)

2025 Critical Updates:
AIMA now requires NISS before residency appointment (April 28, 2025 rule)

VAT threshold raised to €15,000; safety buffer €18,750 (mid-year breach triggers immediate VAT)

Non-EU residents must register for VAT regardless of income (hard rule, no exceptions)

New CAE Rev.4 codes introduced January 2025 (must use correct activity codes)

Advance payment reduced to 65% (from 76.5%, helps Year 2+ cash flow)

Top 5 Risks for Beginners:
Missing NISS before AIMA = application rejection + visa delay

Crossing €18,750 VAT threshold mid-year = immediate VAT + fines if not registered within 15 days

Not filing quarterly social security reports (even at €0 income) = €50–€300 fines per quarter

Late annual IRS filing (after June 30) = €200–€2,500 fine + 4% annual interest

Assuming visa = tax compliance = backdated tax demands + global income declaration obligations
