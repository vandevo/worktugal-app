Step-by-Step Freelancer Onboarding Checklist (Portugal, 2025, income under €40 000)

1. Pre-Setup (Before Working or Invoicing)

Goal: register legally, open required accounts, and avoid early fines.

Step	Action	Why It Matters	Estimated Time	Penalty if Ignored
1	Get a NIF (tax number)	You need it for everything – contracts, invoices, bank, NISS.	Same day at Finanças or Loja do Cidadão	€150–€300 for operating without NIF
2	Open a Portuguese bank account	Required for payments and tax transactions.	1 week average	Transactions may be rejected
3	Obtain NISS (social-security number)	Enables your contributions and benefits.	2 weeks	Delays social-security registration
4	Register activity (“Abertura de Atividade”)	Declares you as self-employed under Regime Simplificado.	10 min online at Finanças	€300–€7 500 fine for unregistered work
2. Getting Paid and Staying Compliant

Goal: start invoicing correctly and track VAT exposure.

Step	Action	When	Notes	Penalty
5	Activate and issue “Recibos Verdes”	From first client payment	Done via Portal das Finanças	€50–€300 late-invoice fines
6	Monitor VAT threshold (€15 000/year)	Monthly or quarterly	Below → exempt (Art. 53); above → register for IVA within 30 days	€200–€2 500 fine for late VAT registration
7	File quarterly Social-Security declarations	Jan 20, Apr 20, Jul 20, Oct 20	Pay 21.4 % of declared income	10 % interest + penalties if late
8	Keep basic records	Always	Income log, invoices, expense receipts	Up to €2 500 + interest for missing proof
3. Tax Filing (IRS 2026 for 2025 income)

Goal: report yearly income correctly and pay what’s due.

Step	Action	When	What Happens
9	Calculate taxable income (75 % rule)	After year-end	Example: €40 000 × 75 % = €30 000 taxable
10	Apply deductions & rates	Standard deduction ~ €4 349 → tax ≈ €5 700	
11	Include social-security contribution (~€6 000)	Year 2+	Total tax burden ≈ €11 700
12	Submit IRS Modelo 3	Apr 1 – Jun 30 2026	File online via Portal das Finanças
13	Pay or receive refund	After assessment	Missed filing = €200–€2 500 fine
4. Routine Maintenance

Goal: stay compliant through automation and awareness.

Frequency	Task	Notes
Monthly	Log revenue, issue invoices, store receipts	Use app or Excel
Quarterly	Review VAT status & SS payments	Check threshold drift
Annually	IRS filing, update income estimate	Adjust contributions for next year
5. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Registering too late → Finanças can retro-fine from first income date.

Missing SS deadlines → 10 % interest compounds quickly.

Crossing €15 000 without VAT registration → triggers audit.

Ignoring letters from Finanças → many are automated penalty notices.

Not declaring foreign income → double-tax risk.

6. Ready-to-Use App Flow (UX Summary)

Intro Screen: “Let’s check your tax readiness.”

Section 1: Identity → NIF + NISS.

Section 2: Activity → Open Regime Simplificado.

Section 3: Invoicing → Recibos Verdes + VAT Monitor.

Section 4: Compliance → Social Security + IRS Calendar.

Section 5: Output → Scorecard + Penalties + Action Plan.
