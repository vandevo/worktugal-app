# Portugal Freelancer Compliance Checklist 2025
## Quick Reference for Beginners (Under €50,000/Year)

---

## ✅ BEFORE YOU START WORKING

- [ ] **Get NIF (Número de Identificação Fiscal)**
  - Where: Finanças office, online (Portal das Finanças), or via intermediary
  - Docs needed: Passport/ID, proof of address (rental contract, utility bill, CRUE)
  - Timeline: 3–10 days
  - Cost: Free
  - *If missed: Cannot open bank account or register with tax authorities*

- [ ] **Open Portuguese Bank Account**
  - Where: Any Portuguese bank (ActivoBank, Moey, Revolut PT, etc.)
  - Docs needed: NIF, passport, proof of income, proof of address
  - Timeline: 3–4 weeks (online) or 3–4 days (in-person)
  - Cost: Variable (€0–€150 setup, then €0–€12/month)
  - *If missed: Cannot receive payments or provide IBAN to Finanças*

- [ ] **Register Activity with Finanças (Abrir Atividade)**
  - Where: Portal das Finanças (online) or Finanças office
  - Docs needed: NIF, password for Portal das Finanças
  - What you declare: Activity start date, activity codes (CIRS for services), estimated annual income, whether you expect VAT
  - **Choose tax regime: Simplified (Regime Simplificado)** ← Most freelancers pick this
  - Timeline: Immediate (online) or same day (in-person)
  - Cost: Free
  - *If missed: Cannot legally issue invoices/recibos verdes; subject to fines €300–€3,750*

- [ ] **Get NISS (Número de Identificação da Segurança Social)**
  - Where: Segurança Social Direta (seg-social.pt) – online only
  - Docs needed: NIF, proof of employment (activity registration from Finanças), proof of address
  - Timeline: 3–4 weeks
  - Cost: Free
  - **⚠ CRITICAL: If you have a visa (D8, D7, etc.), AIMA requires NISS before your residency appointment (rule effective April 28, 2025)**
  - *If missed: AIMA appointment rejected; fines €150–€300 if late; delays visa processing*

## 📅 ONGOING COMPLIANCE (January–December 2025)

### Quarterly Social Security Reports
**Due:** 20th of month following quarter end

- [ ] **Q1 Report (Jan–Mar)** – Due **April 20, 2025**
- [ ] **Q2 Report (Apr–Jun)** – Due **July 20, 2025**
- [ ] **Q3 Report (Jul–Sep)** – Due **October 20, 2025**
- [ ] **Q4 Report (Oct–Dec)** – Due **January 20, 2026**

**What to do:**
- Log into Segurança Social Direta (seg-social.pt)
- Report your gross income for that quarter (even if €0)
- Submit online
- Year 1: No payment due (exempt from contributions, but reporting is mandatory)

**If missed:**
- Penalty: €50–€300 per late quarter
- Impact: May affect future benefits, pension contributions, unemployment eligibility

---

### Issue Recibos Verdes (Green Receipts) for Every Client Payment

**When:** Every time you receive payment from a client

**Where:** Portal das Finanças > "Faturas e Recibos Verdes" > "Emitir"

**What to include:**
- Your NIF
- Service description
- Amount received
- Client's NIF (or country + tax ID if foreign)
- Service date

**Important rules:**
- Must be issued through Portal das Finanças (electronic only)
- Cannot be canceled if issued to a foreign NIF—verify all data first
- Must keep records for minimum 6 years
- Annual total of all receipts = your declared income

**If wrong/missing:**
- Penalty: €300–€3,750 per incorrect receipt
- Impact: Audits, back taxes, social security recalculation

### Monitor Income for VAT Threshold

**Track your annual gross turnover continuously**

| Turnover Level | Action | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| ≤ €15,000 by Dec 31 | Stay VAT-exempt for full 2025 | N/A |
| €15,001–€18,750 in 2025 | Remain exempt for 2025; register for VAT by Jan 31, 2026 | Jan 31, 2026 |
| Exceed €18,750 mid-year (e.g., June) | VAT applies immediately on that invoice; file declaration of alterations within 15 days | 15 days after breach |
| Non-EU resident | Mandatory VAT registration regardless of income | Before first invoice |

**If you miss the deadline:**
- Penalty: €300–€7,500 + back taxes + 30–100% penalty on late payments
- Impact: Cannot issue invoices without VAT; clients may refuse payment

## 📝 YEAR-END & TAX FILING (April–June 2026 for 2025 Income)

- [ ] **File Annual IRS Tax Return**
  - When: **April 1–June 30, 2026** (for 2025 income)
  - Where: Online via Portal das Finanças (Modelo 3) or with certified accountant
  - What to report: Total gross income from recibos verdes, worldwide income if tax resident, foreign bank accounts (if applicable)
  - Cost: Free (DIY) or €200–€500 (if accountant)
  - If missed: €200–€2,500 fine + 4% annual interest on tax owed

- [ ] **If VAT-Registered: File VAT Returns**
  - When: Monthly (by 10th of following month) or quarterly (by 15th of 2nd month after quarter)
  - Where: Portal das Finanças
  - What to report: Output VAT (charged to clients), Input VAT (paid on purchases), net due or refund
  - If missed: €300–€7,500 fine + back taxes

## ❓ COMMON QUESTIONS

**Q: Do I have to pay social security in Year 1?**
A: No. You are exempt from paying contributions for your first 12 months. However, you must still file quarterly income reports to Segurança Social. After 12 months, contributions are 21.4% on 70% of your declared income.

**Q: What if my income is €0 in a quarter?**
A: You still file the report. A zero-income report is normal and does not trigger penalties. However, you must submit it to remain compliant.

**Q: What happens if I exceed €15,000 on June 30?**
A: If you cross €18,750 (the 25% buffer) mid-year, VAT applies immediately on that invoice. You must file a "declaration of alterations" (alterações) within 15 days to register for VAT.

**Q: Am I a tax resident even if I haven't registered?**
A: Yes. If you spend 183+ days in Portugal (or have a permanent home there), you become a tax resident automatically—regardless of registration. This means you must declare worldwide income to Portugal. Registration is not what triggers residency; physical presence does.

**Q: What if I earn from clients outside Portugal?**
A: If you are a tax resident in Portugal, you must declare all worldwide income in your annual IRS return (Annex J for foreign income). You are not charged VAT on services to foreign clients (these use reverse-charge code M40 on recibos verdes).

**Q: Can I switch from Simplified Regime to Organized Regime?**
A: Yes, but only once per year (by end of March) and you must stick to the new regime for 3 years. Organized regime is useful if your actual expenses exceed 25% of income.

**Q: What if I make a mistake on a recibo verde?**
A: If issued to a Portuguese NIF, you can cancel and reissue. If issued to a foreign NIF, it cannot be canceled. Always verify data before finalizing.

**Q: When do I pay my taxes?**
A: IRS tax (income tax) is typically paid in three installments: July, September, and December (based on the previous year's assessment). VAT (if registered) is due 7 days after the reporting deadline (10th or 15th of month, depending on monthly/quarterly filing).

**Q: Do I need an accountant?**
A: For the Simplified Regime under €200,000 income, no—legally not required. However, hiring a certified accountant (€200–€500/year) can prevent costly mistakes, ensure compliance, and optimize deductions.

## 💡 PRO TIPS FOR STAYING COMPLIANT

1. **Set Calendar Reminders:** Mark all quarterly deadlines (April 20, July 20, Oct 20, Jan 20) in your phone now.

2. **Track Income Weekly:** Use a simple spreadsheet to log all invoices. Sum quarterly and annual totals to catch VAT threshold breaches early.

3. **Keep Recibos Verdes Organized:** Save a copy of every receipt you issue. Group by quarter for easy reporting.

4. **Use Accounting Software:** Tools like Rauva, InvoiceXpress, or Moloni automate recibo verde issuance, quarterly reports, and tax calculations. Cost ~€20–€50/month but saves time and errors.

5. **Consult Early, Not Late:** If unsure about VAT, activity codes, or tax residency, book a 45-minute tax consultation (€80–€100) from a Portuguese accountant. It's cheaper than penalties.

6. **Monitor Double Taxation Treaties:** If you also pay taxes in another country (e.g., US, UK, Spain), claim relief through double taxation agreements. Portugal has these with most countries.

7. **Backup Your Documents:** Keep scans of your NIF, NISS, bank account proof, and lease agreement in a secure cloud storage. You'll need these for audits or future applications.
