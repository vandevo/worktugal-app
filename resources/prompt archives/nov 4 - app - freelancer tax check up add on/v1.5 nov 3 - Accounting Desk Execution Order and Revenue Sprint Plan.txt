1. Attention is a means, not an asset.

2. Attention burns energy.
Every comment, DM, and post creates micro-loops of obligation. If there’s no sale or system behind it, it drains you faster than it feeds you.

3. Attention can distort judgment.
You start optimizing for applause instead of truth. You pick what sounds viral instead of what solves pain. That kills clarity and authority.

4. Real power comes from conversion moments.
One checkout link clicked, one invoice paid, one subscription started — those are proof loops. Proof loops compound. Views don’t.

5. Silent builders always win longer.
The ones who look invisible are often stacking systems quietly while everyone else screams online. When they go public, it’s to announce results, not to beg for relevance.
