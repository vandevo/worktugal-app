# Competitive Landscape Mapping

This is a clear overview of the main players and how Worktugal fits into the Portuguese market.  
Every statement is grounded in verifiable information from official sites.

---

# 1. Software competitors (tax and invoicing tools)

## FIZ (Portugal)

**What they do**  
A freelancer focused SaaS offering unlimited certified invoicing via Web, App and WhatsApp plus automatic VAT and Social Security submissions.  
They offer a Tax Shield guarantee that covers fines up to €1 500 per declaration.  
Human and AI support respond at any time.

**Evidence**  
fiz.co

**Gaps vs Worktugal**  
FIZ assumes the user is already compliant.  
No pre filing compliance audit.  
No risk detection.  
No cross border guidance.  
No marketplace.

---

## Rauva (Portugal)

**What they do**  
A digital business banking platform letting users create companies online, open business accounts and manage invoices, expenses and cards.  
Invoices follow Portuguese tax standards.  
Offers transaction syncing, cash flow analytics and one to one support.  
Partners with Swan (an e money institution).  
Not a bank or law firm.

**Evidence**  
get.rauva.com

**Gaps vs Worktugal**  
Offers banking and invoicing, not compliance.  
Assumes users are already legal entities.  
No risk engine.  
No residency or visa guidance.  
Targets SMEs rather than freelancers.

---

## Contasimple (Spain)

**What they do**  
Cloud invoicing and billing for freelancers and small businesses in Spain.  
Ensures e invoice compliance, traceability and automatic submission to Spanish tax authorities.  
Includes OCR expense tracking, bank reconciliation and automated tax calculation.

**Evidence**  
getapp.com

**Gaps vs Worktugal**  
Spain only.  
No diagnostic engine.  
No cross border support.

---

## Taxfix (Germany, Spain, UK)

**What they do**  
A mobile and web app replacing tax forms with simple questions.  
Automatically generates and submits the tax return.  
Shows instant refund calculation.

**Evidence**  
support.taxfix.de

**Gaps vs Worktugal**  
Not suitable for self employed people.  
Not suitable for users with foreign income.  
Focuses on personal income tax.  
No VAT, Social Security or residency guidance.  
No VAT threshold warnings.  
Portugal not supported.

---

## Quipu (Spain)

**What they do**  
An all in one invoicing and cash flow management system.  
Creates and sends invoices, tracks payment status and connects bank accounts.  
Automatically classifies transactions and simulates cash flow.

**Evidence**  
getquipu.com

**Gaps vs Worktugal**  
Focuses on automation.  
Spain only.  
No pre registration compliance checks.  
No migration or residency layer.

---

## Moloni (Portugal)

**What they do**  
A leading online invoicing platform certified by the Portuguese tax authority.  
Emphasises speed, advanced tools, mobile apps and strong security.  
Supports e invoicing, digital signatures and automatic ATCUD codes.

**Evidence**  
moloni.pt

**Gaps vs Worktugal**  
Pure invoicing.  
Assumes users are already compliant.  
No risk scoring.  
No immigration or residency guidance.

---

## InvoiceXpress (Portugal)

**What they do**  
Certified invoicing SaaS offering dashboards, VAT comparison, recurring invoices, SMS and email delivery, SAF T export and advanced search tools.  
Strong security and digital signed PDF invoices.

**Evidence**  
invoicexpress.com

**Gaps vs Worktugal**  
Focus on billing automation.  
No compliance diagnosis.  
No cross border or residency support.  
No marketplace.

# 2. Traditional accounting firms and communities

Regional accounting firms.  
Human driven bookkeeping and tax filing.  
Not cloud native.  
No automated risk scoring.

## ExpatsPortugal services  
A community forum connecting newcomers to service providers.  
Not software.  
No automated compliance systems.

# 3. Worktugal’s wedge

## Pre compliance diagnostic engine  
Worktugal runs an Eleven Rule compliance scan before the user invoices anyone.  
Identifies missing NIF and NISS, unregistered activity, VAT threshold crossings and visa related traps.  
Competitors assume these problems are already solved.

## Timing and behavioral layer  
Worktugal maps Portugal’s fiscal clock, including AT slowdowns, blackout periods, holiday delays and penalty cycles.  
None of the listed competitors offer timing intelligence.

## Cross border focus  
Worktugal targets remote workers and migrants using visas like NHR, D7 and the digital nomad visa.  
Incorporates foreign income rules and residency traps.  
Taxfix explicitly excludes these cases.  
Other tools serve only domestic freelancers.

## Marketplace and partner network  
Worktugal converts diagnostic results into paid Fix Sessions and matches users with vetted accountants.  
FIZ does filings in house.  
Rauva provides banking.  
Moloni and InvoiceXpress provide invoicing.  
No one operates a shared revenue marketplace.

## Portable system architecture  
Worktugal sits before invoicing, banking or filing.  
Its rules engine and fiscal calendar can be replicated in Spain, Netherlands or UAE.  
Competitors are tied to national filings or banking licenses.

---

# Conclusion

FIZ, Rauva, Moloni and InvoiceXpress prove there is demand for automation.  
But they only start after the user is already compliant.

Worktugal intercepts users before they enter the system.  
It detects risks, maps deadlines, prevents fines and connects users to the right accountant.  
This position is not occupied by any invoicing tool, banking app or traditional firm.

Worktugal therefore owns a unique wedge: a compliance risk and timing intelligence engine that transforms chaos into clear actions before any invoice is sent.
