• Return consult, 60 minutes session, 10 minutes buffer, 70 minutes total. Two consults equals two hours and twenty minutes.
• Total about nineteen hours and fifty minutes. One strong accountant can do twenty to twenty five billable hours per week. Two gives you headroom, faster scheduling, and coverage for illness.

• Each accountant opens two daily triage slots on three weekdays, for example Tue, Wed, Thu. That is twelve triage slots across two providers.
• Add two Saturday slots shared between them for people who work weekdays.
• You now have fourteen triage slots. Overflows can go to the standby.

Target window for start pack kickoff, within three business days
• Each accountant opens three kickoff slots in a week, ninety minutes each.
• Six kickoff slots covers five booked start packs with one spare.

Provider A, Tue and Thu late afternoon triage, Wed morning kickoffs.
Provider B, Mon afternoon triage, Wed afternoon triage, Fri morning kickoffs.
Standby C, Sat morning triage, Thu evening triage backup, one floating kickoff.

• More than three business days average wait for a kickoff slot.
