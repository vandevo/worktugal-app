# Thread Handover Protocol

## 1. Thread Overview

Scope: This session covered two major workstreams. First, building an auto-loading AI governance layer in `.cursor/rules/` so cheaper models (Gemini Flash 3.1) can follow Opus 4.6's established patterns, constraints, and architecture without manual prompt loading. Second, a full platform route consolidation that killed the legacy `/checkup` and `/compliance-review` routes, redirected everything to `/diagnostic`, updated all user-facing copy (homepage SEO, footer, navigation, FAQ, changelog) to match the new "Compliance Risk Diagnostic" product narrative, and added optional contact fields to the diagnostic form.

Strategic drivers: Van is bootstrapping solo, cannot afford daily Opus usage, and needs cheaper models to execute safely within established guardrails. The prior Gemini codebase destruction incident (v1.3.5 revert) made protective rules essential. The platform had three competing product routes confusing users and diluting SEO. Revenue pressure requires a clean, single funnel pointing to the 29 EUR paid risk scan.

Outcome: The platform now has a single diagnostic funnel at `/diagnostic`, a governance rules system that auto-loads on every AI session, all user-facing pages aligned to "hidden compliance risk detection" positioning, and an updated changelog entry (v2.0) in Supabase. The codebase is clean with zero TypeScript or lint errors. Phase 2.5 and Phase 4 of the master plan are complete. Next milestone is production deploy to start collecting real user submissions.

Decision: Created 4 auto-loading `.cursor/rules/*.mdc` governance files instead of a single master prompt.
